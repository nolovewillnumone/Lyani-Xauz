One of the central squares of the city of Bukhara, an architectural ensemble created in the 16th - 17th centuries. The area of ​​the formed buildings of the Kukeldash madrasah (Bukhara), the Divan-Begi madrasah and the Divan-Begi khanaka are grouped around the reservoir of the Nadir-Begi house. The dimensions of the ensemble are approximately 150 by 200 meters. In the Middle Ages, Lyabi-Khauz was a conventional trading area, this created proximity to the main trading point with its trading domes (one of them was located 200 m from the square) and extreme crowding of buildings in Bukhara, so Lyabi-Khauz was one of the first open an open area.
